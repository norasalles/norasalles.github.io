Payment Options

Path: Setup > Site Preferences > Payment Options

You can use the Payment Options page to configure:

  • Charge cards
  • Multiple charge cards
  • Payment override charge cards

For more information, click one of the following:

Charge Card

Path: Setup > Site Preferences > Payment Options | Charge Card tab

The Charge Card tab controls payment option settings so that if the GDS returns an error during the booking process regarding an invalid charge card number or expiration date, the booking is stopped and the system displays an error message to the user.

You can also allow users to have the option to phone-in information regarding their form of payment for the flight portion of their booking.

To configure payment options

  1. Select whether to Stop the booking if the GDS detects a problem with the charge card number or expiration date.
  2. Select whether to Display Phone-in Information as an alternate form of payment.

    Notes   

    • To use this feature, you must have Multiple Charge Card functionality enabled.
    • Phone-in Information is not accepted for hotel reservations as a credit card is required for all hotel bookings.

    If you select this option, do the following:

    1. Optional: Enter a message you want displayed when users select Phone-in Information on the Trip review and checkout page. This information is also displayed in a pop-up message when travelers purchase a trip.
    2. In the Phone-in Time Limit list, click the maximum number of minutes users have to call and report the form of payment before their reservation is canceled. The message displayed to the user is: "Phone-in bookings will be cancelled XX minutes from their receipt.” The time limit notice is repeated in the popup message displayed when they click Purchase Trip: "You must call the ticket center within XX minutes to complete the booking."
    3. In the Phone Number box, type the phone number users should call to report their form of payment. The message displayed before the number is: “Phone-in charge card info to the following phone number:” The message and number are repeated in the pop-up message displayed when they click Purchase Trip.
  3. Click Save.

Multiple Charge Card Administration

Path: Setup > Site Preferences > Payment Options | Multiple Charge Card Administration tab

The Multiple Charge Card Administration tab controls the configuration for multiple charge cards. This link will appear only if GetThere staff has enabled Multiple Charge Card functionality.

The Multiple Charge Card feature lets you allow users to enter up to eight personal charge cards. You can also add up to four central billing cards (also called site cards). Central billing cards are company cards that are used to pay for airfare, Air Extras, rail, or hotel guarantees. The charges are automatically billed to the company and not the user.

Note  To enable this feature, contact your service representative.

For more information, click one of the following:

  • Configure Multiple Charge Cards
    1. Select how to Limit the traveler's form of payment for airline reservations. If you select no limits, travelers can use either personal credit cards or central billing cards to pay for airline tickets. Otherwise, you can limit the type of credit card to user cards or central billing cards.

      Note  The default setting is: no limits.

    2. Select whether to hide all payment options other than the default when a default payment option is specified for air travel, unless that card is prohibited by the Limit the traveler's form of payment for airline reservations to list above.
    3. Select how to Limit the traveler's form of payment for air extras. If you select no limits, travelers can use either personal credit cards or central billing cards to pay for Air Extras. Otherwise, you can limit the type of credit card to user cards or central billing cards.

      Note  The default setting is: no limits.

    4. Select whether to hide all payment options other than the default when a default payment option is specified for air extras, unless that card is prohibited by the Limit the traveler's form of payment for air extras to list above.
    5. Select how to Limit the traveler's form of payment for hotel guarantees. If you select no limits, travelers can use either personal credit cards or central billing cards to guarantee hotel reservations. Otherwise, you can limit the type of credit card to user cards or central billing cards.

      Note  The default setting is: no limits.

    6. Select whether to hide all payment options other than the default when a default payment option is specified for hotel guarantees, unless that card is prohibited by the Limit the traveler's form of payment for hotel guarantees to list above.

    7. Select how to Limit the traveler's form of payment for rail reservations. If you select do not allow a rail card, the Profile page does not display the Charge Card to Use for Rail list, so the user cannot set up a rail-specific charge card. If you select no limits, travelers can use either personal credit cards or central billing cards to pay for rail tickets. Otherwise, you can limit the type of credit card to user cards or central billing cards.

      Notes   

      • The default setting is: do not allow a rail card.
      • If the traveler chooses to pay for the ticket at the train station, the charge card the traveler uses at the station is the card that will be charged.
    8. Select whether to hide all payment options other than the default when a default payment option is specified for rail travel, unless that card is prohibited by the Limit the traveler's form of payment for rail reservations to list above.

    9. Select Which cards can the traveler use for airline reservations.

      Notes  

      • The default and recommended setting is: hold down the Ctrl key while you click to highlight all card options.
      • If you limit the cards, then travelers can only use the cards you specify to pay for airline reservations.

    10. Select Which cards can the traveler use for hotel guarantees.

      Notes   

      • The default and recommended setting is: All
      • If you limit the cards, then travelers can only use the cards you specify to guarantee hotel reservations.

    11. Select Which cards can the traveler use for rail reservations.

      Notes  

      • The default and recommended setting is: All
      • If you limit the cards, then travelers can only use the cards you specify to guarantee rail reservations.

    12. Select What is the total number of unique charge cards permitted per traveler (for both airline and hotel).

      Notes   

      • The default setting is: 8
      • It is not a good practice to make changes to this setting once you have configured it because end users may be confused if they cannot find information they previously entered into the system.
      • Information that has been entered and not deleted, even though it is not displayed to the end user, is still used by the system. Even though Air or Hotel reservations may require only certain types of charge cards, that does not prevent users from entering other types of cards in their Profile.
    13. Select whether to Allow users to purchase airline reservations using a temporary charge card. This toggle also controls whether to show or not show Temporary Card as a billing option for air reservations on the Trip review and checkout page.
    14. Select whether to Allow users to purchase Air Extras using a temporary charge card. This toggle also controls whether to show or not show Temporary Card as a billing option for Air Extras on the Trip review and checkout page.
    15. Click Save.
  • Add a Site Card
    1. On the Multiple Charge Card Administration tab, click Add.
    2. In the Unique Label box, type the name that is displayed to users.
    3. In the Payment Type list, click Site Card.
    4. In the Card Type list, click the card type.

      Note  The default setting is: American Express.

    5. In the Add to User Form of Payment List, click the type of reservations for which the credit card can be used.
    6. Type the Card number. The number is displayed as ***** or as ON FILE.
    7. Type the Expiration Date in MM/YY format. The expiration date is displayed as ***** or as ON FILE.

    8. If you are using Air Connect Auto Booking vendors that contain an asterisk or VIA Rail, enter the following information to support bookings made with Site Cards:

      Note  The address must be complete for these vendors to accept the form of payment.

      1. In the Name on Card box, type a first and last name or a two word company name (e.g. ABC Company). You can enter up to 65 characters.

        Note  If no name is listed, the travelers first and last name are sent. If multiple travelers exist, the first traveler's name is sent. If a single name exists, the booking fails.

      2. In the Billing Country list, click the billing country for the site card.
      3. In the Billing Address and Billing Address 2 boxes, type the billing address for the site card. You can enter up to 64 characters in each box.
      4. In the Billing City and Billing State/Province boxes, type the billing city and state/province for the site card. You can enter up to 32 characters in each box.
      5. In the Billing Zip Code box, type the billing zip code for the site card. You can enter up to 16 characters.
    9. Click Save.
  • Add a Virtual Card
    1. On the Multiple Charge Card Administration tab, click Add.
    2. In the Unique Label box, type the name that is displayed to users.
    3. In the Payment Type list, click Virtual Card.
    4. In the Card Type list, click the card type.

      Note  The default setting is: American Express.

    5. In the Add to User Form of Payment List, click the type of reservations for which the credit card can be used.

      Note  For virtual cards, you can currently only select hotel.

    6. In the Virtual Card Vendor list, click the virtual card vendor for the virtual cards travelers can use.

      Note  For virtual cards, you can currently only select Conferma.

    7. Type the Consumer Account Code provided by Conferma.
    8. Type the Agent ID provided by Conferma.
    9. Type the Booker ID provided by Conferma.
    10. In the Failed Fax Email box, type the e-mail that Conferma should use to communicate when there is a failure to fax a hotel.

    11. In the Payment Instruction box, type the information to include on the fax sent to hotel to indicate the allowable charges for the card.

      Note  If the card is new and you enter more than 200 characters, an error appears, and the payment instructions will be blank. If you are editing the payment instructions for an existing card and you enter more than 200 characters, an error appears and the payment instructions return to what you entered previously.

    12. If you are using Air Connect Auto Booking vendors that contain an asterisk or VIA Rail, enter the following information to support bookings made with Site Cards:

      Note  The address must be complete for these vendors to accept the form of payment.

      1. In the Name on Card box, type a first and last name or a two word company name (e.g. ABC Company). You can enter up to 65 characters.

        Note  If no name is listed, the travelers first and last name are sent. If multiple travelers exist, the first traveler's name is sent. If a single name exists, the booking fails.

      2. In the Billing Country list, click the billing country for the site card.
      3. In the Billing Address and Billing Address 2 boxes, type the billing address for the site card. You can enter up to 64 characters in each box.
      4. In the Billing City and Billing State/Province boxes, type the billing city and state/province for the site card. You can enter up to 32 characters in each box.
      5. In the Billing Zip Code box, type the billing zip code for the site card. You can enter up to 16 characters.

    13. Click Save.

      Notes   

      • If a site allows users to book multiple hotel rooms in a hotel, the site must select the Allow users to request multiple hotel room types in a hotel on the Hotel Search page. This ensures that a hotel booking is made per room selected because a hotel booking can only contain one room when a virtual card in used.
      • For sites that require Pre-Trip approval, the system does not cancel any decline reservations. The agency is responsible for canceling reservations and any virtual cards that are a part of the reservation.
      • If the user selects a hotel connect property, virtual cards will not be offered in the hotel billing information section.
      • If the user selects a hotel that cannot be booked on-line and then selects the option for I want to add this hotel to my itinerary and allow a travel agent to book it for me., because the card selected is used for all hotels, virtual cards will not be offered in the hotel billing information section.
      •  
  • Configure a Virtual Card

    • The main site name and subsite name are always sent to the virtual card vendor. The 8 default fields will also be reported when using GetThere on smartphones. However, you can also send additional fields.

    1. In the Custom Data Fields column, click the Configure Fields link for the virtual card.

      The Configure Fields page appears for the virtual card.

    2. Do one of the following:

      To

      Do

      Add a custom data field

      Note  You can add up to 120 custom data fields.

      1. Click Add.
      2. In the Field Label box, type a label for the custom data field. You can enter up to 50 characters. The label must be unique for the virtual card.
      3. In the Field Expression box, type the field expression for retrieving the data and sending the data as follows:
        • The LVIS tags used for PNR Editor Strings should be used for Field Expressions.
        • If the result for the configured field expression is greater than 200, the data will be truncated to 200 characters and sent to Conferma.
        • JavaScript is not allowed.
        • The following is list of field expressions that have been created specifically for reporting. These should not be used in PNR Editor Strings.
          • itin.hotel.chain
          • itin.hotel.checkinDate
          • itin.hotel.checkoutDate
          • itin.hotel.ssr.count
          • itin.hotel.ssr
          • itin.hotel.room_count
          • itin.hotel.property_id
          • itin.hotel.rate
          • itin.hotel.address
          • itin.hotel.phone_number
          • itin.hotel.room_desc

          There are corresponding PNR editor tags that are available, and they should not be configured in a field expression.

      4. Click Save.

      Edit a custom data field

      1. In the Field Label column, click the link of the field you want edit.
      2. Change the field label and/or field expression as needed.
      3. Click Save.

      Delete a custom data field

      1. In the first column, select the check boxes for the custom data fields you want to delete.

      2. Click Delete.

        A confirmation dialog appears.

      3. Click OK.
    3. Click Save.
  • Edit a Site or Virtual Card
    1. In the Label column, click the name of the card that you want to edit.
    2. Edit the site card information as needed.
    3. Click Save.
  • Delete Site or Virtual Cards
    1. In the Edit Site Cards table, select the check boxes for the site cards you want to delete.

    2. Click Delete.

      A confirmation dialog box appears.

    3. Click OK.
  • Delete All Site or Virtual Cards
    1. Select the check box in the header row.

    2. Click Delete.

      A confirmation dialog box appears.

    3. Click OK.

Payment Override Charge Cards

Path: Setup > Site Preferences > Payment Options | Payment Override Charge Cards tab

You can use the Payment Override Charge Cards tab to add Payment Override Charge Cards that are used in PNR editor strings to override site and profile cards. These cards do not display as payment options to customers.

  • Add a Payment Override Charge Card
    1. On the Payment Override Charge Cards tab, click Add.
    2. In the Unique Label box, type the name that is displayed to users.
    3. In the Card Type list, click the card type.

      Note  The default setting is: American Express.

    4. Type the Card number. The number is displayed as ***** or as ON FILE.
    5. Type the Expiration Date in MM/YY format. The expiration date is displayed as ***** or as ON FILE.
    6. Click Save.
  • Edit a Payment Override Charge Card
    1. In the Name column, click the name of the card you want to edit.
    2. Edit the payment override charge card information as needed.
    3. Click Save.
  • Delete Payment Override Charge Cards
    1. In the table of payment override charge cards, select the check boxes for the payment override charge cards you want to delete.

    2. Click Delete.

      A confirmation dialog box appears.

    3. Click OK.
  • Delete All Payment Override Charge Cards
    1. Select the check box in the header row.

    2. Click Delete.

      A confirmation dialog box appears.

    3. Click OK.
