Air Connect – Introduction

This feature, when enabled, allows GetThere® users to compare flight options and book itineraries utilizing published and negotiated airfares as found in the GDS, as well as fares found on air travel sites.  It provides Travel Managers with the ability to strategically manage and control the display and usage of Web fares. 

The Air Connect Feature Includes

  • Over 75 air travel sites to choose (Find the updated carrier list at the appendix)

  • Up to 50 sites can be configured. System will show an error message if adding carrier exceeds the number 50.

  • Auto Book method only. Mediate and Link booking method has been discontinued.

  • Ability to support frequent flyer numbers for Air Connect vendors

The Site Administrator Also Has Control over the Following

  • Which supplier sites are used and how they are accessed?

  • Whether the Traveler has to request Web fares or if they are automatically displayed

  • The amount of time allocated for the airline Web sites to return information

  • The markets in which Web fares are displayed

  • The savings the Traveler must achieve when displaying a Web fare over a GDS fare

  • The messages displayed to Travelers throughout the booking process and on the confirmation e-mail (e-mail for Auto Bookings only)

  • Whether or not passive segments are added to GDS PNRs for GetThere Air Connect bookings (Auto Bookings only)

  • Where Air Connect reservations are queued if applicable (Auto bookings only)

For Travelers utilizing standard Home page functionality, Web fares airfare information is displayed in addition to the normal GDS fares.  Fares from the travel websites are displayed on the Select a flight pages when shopping by price as well as the Review low fare options page when shopping by schedule.

Note    In order for travelers to book Air Connect fares, the travelers must enter their full name and billing address for the credit card used for payment.

Restrictions

  • Applicable to Shop by price and Shop by schedule.

  • Unable to complete reservations with mixed carriers.

  • Applicable only to coach/lowest available requests.

  • Applicable to one way and round trips only.

Note    Multiple destination itineraries or Travel Arranger trip Duplication requests are not supported.

  • Changes and cancellations are not supported at this time.

  • Unable to support Air Connect itineraries using AAirPass.

  • Unable to modify air segments after reservation is complete.

  • Frequent Traveler Programs and Special requests are not supported at this time.

  • Plus/minus days functionality is not supported.

  • Unused Tickets functionality is not supported.

  • Offline changes are not reflected in GetThere.

  • Alternate airports functionality is not supported.

 

Payment Authentication – Strong Customer Authentication (SCA)

On 14 September 2019, new requirements for authenticating online payments will be introduced in Europe as part of the second Payment Services Directive (PSD2).

 

Strong Customer Authentication (SCA) is a new European regulatory requirement to reduce fraud and make online payments more secure. To accept payments once SCA goes into effect, you will need to build additional authentication into your checkout flow. SCA requires authentication to use at least two of the following three elements.

 

 

The GetThere booking process has been enhanced to support SCA when multi-factor authentication is required to confirm payment for the transaction.  When the booking is submitted from the Checkout page the traveler will be redirected to an authentication process hosted by their payment provider for the booking.  Once the authentication steps are complete the traveler will automatically be redirected back to GetThere where the booking process will resume as normal (at the step between the Checkout page and the Reservation complete page).

 

Applicability:

 

No setup or configuration steps are required to enable SCA within GetThere. Extra customer authentication will not always take place.  The Strong Customer Authentication process will only be triggered when all of the following situations apply:

 

  • AirConnect must be enabled in the site.

  • Only applies to TravelFusion airlines in Europe.

  • The itinerary includes an Air Connect booking using TravelFusion.

  • The Air Connect airline is NOT setup as TFPay or Direct Bill with TravelFusion.

  • Both the Air Connect airline and the payment provider (i.e., cardholder’s bank) are located in Europe (in the EEA).

  • The transaction value is above a specific threshold (approximately €30 euros or £26 pounds, except in cases where multiple low-value transactions precede a subsequent request).

  • The specific payment provider’s real-time risk analysis algorithms indicate the additional authentication is required.

Reservation Process:

With Auto Booking, the reservation process is seamless to the Traveler.  They stay within the GetThere booking tool and when the Traveler clicks the Select button for the itinerary they prefer, they are taken to the Select a hotel or Trip review and checkout page (whichever is applicable).  The booking is completed when the Traveler clicks the Purchase trip button.  The completed reservation can be added to the GDS as a passive segment and the reservation can be viewed under Trips.

Note: Travel Policy configurations are applied to Auto Booking only reservations.  Since Air Connect can be configured with a different flight availability time window, some options may not be called out of policy since their departure/arrival times may be outside of the Travel Policy's evaluation window.

Goods and Services Tax (India)

The government of India has adopted a new regime on Goods and Services Tax (GST) implemented on July 1, 2017. GST is a tax that merges many individually applied taxes into a single tax. It is a comprehensive indirect tax on manufacture, sale, and consumption of goods and services throughout India. Travel is categorized as “Goods and Services.”

For any tickets issued worldwide where travel originates in India, regardless of point of sale, the airline must send a GST invoice within 30 days of the date of ticket issuance regardless of the form of payment. To allow the airline to send the invoice, the company’s GST information must be entered into the PNR at time of booking.

IF itin.wc_booking_number THEN "#5h-webconnect booking " + itin.carriers. REPLACE["\[",""] ENDIF

* IMPORTANT – Additional Information for Specific Carriers

  • Aer Lingus – Cardholder address cannot exceed 30 characters; otherwise booking fails. Cardholder must provide complete first name and last name: No abbreviations are allowed such as Mr. J Smith, Mr. J. Smith or Mr. Smith. The correct form accepted by the supplier is “Mr. John Smith”.

  • Air Asia – Login credentials are not required. User personal login credentials if available. Otherwise, do not add login information for any Air Asia carrier.

Login credentials required for AirAsia Corporate. Login credentials are optional for AirAsia*.

Air Asia airlines cannot be mixed in a connection with any other AirConnect airlines. Air Asia flight booking always required CVV for the credit card used. BTA and/or Centralize Billing credit card cannot be utilized. AirAsia only allow to mix flight options which also required CVV. Example: AirAsia can be mixed with FireFly.

When shopping, each of the carriers displays the Air Asia logo. On the Trip review and checkout page, the airline name displays as well.

Note    Air Asia does show schedules in the GDS, but pricing and booking are not supported. Therefore, when configuring Air Asia carriers in Air Connect, the best practice is to have a GetThere representative block these carriers.

  • CebuPacificB2B - Direct bill only and must have agency credentials.

 

  • EasyJet Login Credentials – Login credentials are no longer required.  If no login credentials have been added within the user's profile, the system sends the e-mail address entered on the purchase page.  EasyJet verifies that an account does not exist.  If not, EasyJet creates an account and forward password to the e-mail address provided.   If the Agency Default option is selected, the system sends the username and password entered as the login credentials.  Also, EasyJet requires a person's title (e.g. Mr., Mrs., etc.).  If the salutation or title setting is disabled, the system sends the title of Mr. by default.

  • easyJet API Fee – easyJet fares in GetThere are bit of expensive than the easyJet website because easyJet charges API fee of 6GBP per segment.

  • JetStar – JetStar Asia Airways and Valuair content available when JetStar is enabled.

  • Ryan Air passengers must check in online and present their printed boarding pass at the airport. Passengers who do not check in online will be required to pay a fee to be issued a boarding pass at the airport. Ryan Air no longer allows airport check-ins. Ryan Air bookings completed via GetThere include the online check-in fee. Travelers booking through GetThere should print their boarding passes on Ryan Air’s Web site.

GetThere users can now also select business class seats. The seats are automatically assigned. However, if you want a specific seat, you can contact Ryan Air directly to request that seat.  The seat number does not display in GetThere, the seat number is printed on the confirmation sent by Ryan Air.  The class of service appears as "Y" in the GDS passive PNR (not business class).  Seat assignment information is not included in the GDS passive PNR.

Note    Specific seat requests are not allowed for Air Connect carriers.

  • Tiger Airways – Tiger airways requires passport information (passport number, passport expiry date and passport country of issue).

  • Transavia – Passive segments for this vendor are not supported in Worldspan.

  • Wizz Air – Individual user login credentials are required. They must be stored in the profiles.

  • Volaris –  Volaris air booking via Air Connect is required U.S. State information in the delivery and billing address. It is important to update the toggle “What display standard should other display items follow?” to US standard displays at Setup > Site Preferences > Localization > Miscellaneous in order to show the state/province box to the user profile and checkout page.
